Retinal Disorders & Floaters

The retina is the nerve layer at the back of your eye. Retinal disorders may appear as light flashes or black floaters in your field of vision, or blurring or loss of vision in the corners of your sight. People with diabetes are highly susceptible to retinal diseases. A retinal tear or detachment is considered an ocular emergency. Ask Dr. Garza for a dilated retinal exam or an Optomap screening if you suspect a problem. Be wise about your eyes.
